IIT Delhi Highlights 2022

Established in 1961 as College of Engineering in Delhi, IIT Delhi was later declared as an 'Institution of National Importance' and was renamed as 'Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Delhi'. It was then granted the status of a Deemed University with powers to decide its own academic policy, conduct its own examinations, and award its own degrees. It is a public technical and research university that is counted amongst the oldest IITs of India. IITD has been ranked 2 for ‘Engineering’, 4 for ‘Overall’ and 5 under ‘Management’ by NIRF 2021. IITD has 19 Centres of Excellence, out of which four are set up by IIT Delhi, eight are industry-sponsored and seven are funded by sponsored projects. At present, the institute offers more than 85 courses at UG, PG and PhD level. These courses are imparted through 16 departments and six distinct schools of IITD. IIT Delhi is known for its collaborations which include 30 national and 108 international active MoUs. IITD is extending its boundaries with one extension campus being set up at Sonepat and another one soon coming up in Jhajjar, Haryana.
